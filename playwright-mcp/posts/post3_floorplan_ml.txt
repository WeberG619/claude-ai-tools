I'm teaching AI to read architectural floor plans.

Not just OCR. Not just edge detection. Actual spatial understanding -- walls, doors, rooms, dimensions, relationships between spaces.

The pipeline so far:
- 76 training videos processed and annotated
- Custom model at 70% readiness (and climbing)
- PDF and CAD drawings converted into structured data
- Room detection, wall segmentation, opening identification

The end goal is simple: drop a PDF floor plan into the pipeline, get a Revit model out the other side.

Think about what that means. Every existing building with only paper drawings. Every as-built PDF sitting in a project folder. Every napkin sketch that needs to become a real model. All of it becomes machine-readable.

The hard part isn't the ML. It's the architectural domain knowledge. Teaching a model that a dashed line near a wall is a door swing, not a property line. That a small rectangle in a wall is a window, not a column. Context matters, and architecture is full of implicit conventions that humans read effortlessly but machines struggle with.

That's why this needs to be built by someone who's actually worked in BIM, not just in ML.
